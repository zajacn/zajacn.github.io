Research Cycle in Genomics

The 4 week block course for Master's and Bachelor students from UZH and ETH, led by me and Dr. Jonas Grossmann from the Functional Genomics Center Zurich.

Term: Fall

Location: Functional Genomics Center Zurich

Course description

The course was based on an ongoing research project from the group of Prof. Rolf Kümmerli at the UZH in the topic of human bacterial pathogen evolution. At the beginning of the course the students are provided phenotypic, genomic, and proteomic datasets generated during the experiment to formulate their own research question in the field and, in groups of two, conduct analyses based on their own research interests. At the end the students write an abstract and present the results in the form of a poster during a mini symposium. The course is designed to help students develop as independent thinkers, learn the importance of integrating information from different -omics fields to understand complex phenomena, and gain knowledge on bacterial evolution and the state-of-the-art methodologies in genomics and proteomics.

I taught the course together with Dr. Jonas Grossmann from 2021-2024. The course is still ongoing.

Schedule

Week Date Topic Materials
1 Course Introduction

Introduction to Microbiology and Projects happening at the Kümmerli Lab. During that week Jonas gave an introduction into the experiment the data came from, the outline of the course, the expectations etc. I gave the students a short introduction into principles of research-based teaching and learning and philosophy of science. During the first week students form groups and start literature research, sketch out potential topics they are interested in.
2 Dive into the data

Students receive their data and start their research projects. During the second week Jonas gave an introduction into proteomics data analysis and I gave an introductory lecture into a Next Generation Sequencing Experiment (genomics fundamentals, data analysis workflows, data formats, potential outcomes from genomic variant analysis). We assisted the students in focusing their research questions, narrowing down their hypothesis and starting their data analysis.
3 Preparation for a symposium

During the third week students finalise their projects, write abstracts about their research and create posters for a mini-symposium organized in the fourth week. I gave a short lecture on how to write a good abstract and create an effective poster.
4 Mini-Symposium

The students meet at a mini-symposium where they present their findings to the group. A day after they do a day project using all the skills they learnt during the course.
